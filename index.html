The Evolution of New Media:
A Journey from ARPANET to AI

Published November 30, 2025 • 10 minute read

What is New Media?

New media is interactive, digital, and user-generated content that spreads instantly across networks. Unlike old media (TV, radio, newspapers), anyone can now create, share, and remix information — turning every smartphone into a broadcasting station.

The Timeline That Changed Everything

1969 — ARPANET
First internet message crashed after typing “LO”. Only 4 computers were connected!

1991 — World Wide Web
Tim Berners-Lee gave it away for free. First website still works: info.cern.ch

2004–2006 — Web 2.0 Revolution

Ordinary people became creators. YouTube’s first video = 19 seconds.

2007 — iPhone
Internet in your pocket 24/7. Today: more smartphones than toothbrushes!

2016–2020 — Short Video Era
TikTok made 15-second videos rule the world. Attention span dropped to 8 seconds.

2022+ — Artificial Intelligence
ChatGPT, deepfakes, AI art. By 2026, 90% of online content might be made by machines.

Shocking Truth: Fake news spreads 6× faster than real news on social media (MIT, 2018).

Why Media & Information Literacy is Now a Superpower

In 2025, we’re surrounded by deepfakes, filter bubbles, and algorithm-driven feeds. Being able to spot fake videos, verify sources, and protect privacy isn’t just “nice to have” — it’s survival.

Personal Reflection — Jobelle Flores, Grade 12
